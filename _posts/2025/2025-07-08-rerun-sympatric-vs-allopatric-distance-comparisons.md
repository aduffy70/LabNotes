# Problem

The three North American _Sphagnum magellanicum_ complex species with sympatric sites differ in geographic range size, so sympatry/allopatry is confounded with geographic distance.

# Solution

Rerun the sympatric vs allopatric genetic distance comparisons without the outlier sites. Keep only samples of each species from the part of eastern North America where the three species ranges overlap.  I removed:
  * BC Campbell River (_Sphagnum divinum_)
  * WA Bunchgrass Meadows and Sunday Lake Trailhead (_S. divinum_)
  * AB Kirby North and Kirby South (_S. divinum_)
  * MN Spruce (_S. divinum_)
  * WI Beulah Bog (_S. divinum_)
  * MI Westlake Bog (_S. divinum_)
  * LB Schefferville11 (_S. divinum_)
  * QC Schefferville13 (_S. divinum_)
  * NC Cedar Mountain Bog, Highlands Biological Station, Pineola, Sparta, Stones Lake (_Sphagnum diabolicum_)
  * NF La Manche (_S. diabolicum_)
  
I kept Sept Iles with _S. divinum_ and _Sphagnum medium_. It is somewhat of a geographic outlier but has sympatric species.
