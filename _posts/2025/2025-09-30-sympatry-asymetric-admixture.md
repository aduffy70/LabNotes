For the _Sphagnum magellanicum_ complex sympatry project, one of the reviewers questioned what evidence we have for asymetric admixture in _Sphagnum diabolicum_ and _Sphagnum magniae_.

What evidence do we have that there is more _S. diabolicum_ admixture into _S. magniae_ samples than _S. magniae_ admixture into _S. diabolicum_ samples? Specifically using Figure 7 but also Figure 8.

| Species | Pure samples | Admixed samples (>1%) | Total | Average % admixture (of samples that are admixed >1%) |
| ------- | ------------ | --------------------- | ----- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| _S. magniae_ | 12 | 36 | 48 | 15.2% |
| _S. diabolicum_ | 59 | 32 | 91 | 9.0% |

There are more admixed _S. magniae_ samples (>1% in STRUCTURE) than admixed _S. diabolicum_ (36 vs 32) despite there being twice as many _S. diabolicum_ samples overall (48 vs 91).

From Figure 7:

| Species | Mean % admixed SNPs | Median % admixed SNPs |
| ------- | ------------------- | --------------------- |
| _S. magniae_ | 4.7% | 3.1% |
| _S. diabolicum_ | 5.8% | 1.9% |
